
# 🕳️ The Lyer

> "What's a Lyer?"
> The room goes quiet. The cold settles in.

> Internal contributor doc. The Lyer is named plainly here, per `CLAUDE.md`.
> In the fiction and in every player-facing surface it is never named at
> all. Reconciled against the rewritten prose drafts of *The Cabin* and
> *No Further* (July 2026).

---

## Overview

- **The Lyer is ancient, unknown, and wrong.**
  It does not chase. It does not breathe. It lies in wait.
  And it lies.

- **It is never named in the fiction.**
  Not by Elli, not by Nika, not by the village, not by the narration.
  Nika's voicemail line carries the naming: *"It's... it's lying out
  there."* The word "Lyer" exists only on this side of the page, for
  contributors.

- **It is not fully described.**
  Its nature, origins, and form remain elusive: hinted at, never confirmed.
  The less said, the worse it becomes.

---

## Name and Nature

- **The naming (settled):**
  - No one in the fiction says "Lyer". The dual reading ("liar" and "one who
    lies in wait") is carried entirely by Nika's voicemail, and by the
    stories' titles and behaviour.
  - The village refuses to name it. When a man at the bar offers *hiisi*,
    Matti shuts it down with one word. It does not belong to any mythology,
    and the people who live nearest to it will not give it a word at all.
    What they have instead is usage: *still days*, *no further*, *if a place
    wants to be empty, let it*.

- **What it wants:**
  - Willing participation inside a comfortable lie. Not terror. Terror is a
    tool: it herds by fear only to make the warmth land harder.
  - The horror at the end is not pursuit. It is indifference.

---

## Rules (never stated in the fiction, always obeyed)

- **The lie is co-authored.** It holds exactly as well as the victim's
  belief holds. Seams (frost grained like split wood, birch knuckles, the
  late smile, black floorboards) appear where belief leaks, not where the
  Lyer fails. The copy is at its most perfect when Elli is concussed and
  desperate.
- **The knowledge rule.** A copy knows only what its original knows, feels,
  or witnessed, plus anything said aloud to it. It cannot perform a
  relationship it has never seen. This gap is the escape mechanism.
- **Naming does not banish.** Refusal spoils the game; the Lyer withdraws
  attention and releases. No pursuit. The withdrawal feels like a book
  being closed.
- **It is never seen clearly.** Fragments only. Elli refuses three looks:
  after the collision, leaving the false cabin, and in never being shown
  what is under the face.
- **The cost is implied, never articulated.** It has now learnt Elli as it
  learnt Nika. The scraping in the coda is the receipt.

---

## Myth and Behaviour

- **It does not chase.**
  - It waits. It seeps. It corrupts. When it moves someone by fear, the
    running takes them exactly where it wanted them.
  - It arranges: chairs squared to a table, a fire tended for hours, a book
    left open, a forest walked thirty metres sideways.

- **Its influence is felt before it is seen.**
  - Cold that comes up from the ground and starts below the skin.
  - Stillness beyond silence. Breath that stops showing. Sound with the
    grain taken out of it.
  - Distances stop agreeing with themselves. Paths measure differently on
    different days. Light fails an hour before it has any right to.

- **It cannot be researched or exorcised.**
  - No grimoires, no folkloric rituals, no mythology that claims it.
  - What survives is practice, not knowledge: the 1934 ledger's *no
    further*, washing brought in on still days, a place let be empty.

## Effects of Its Presence

- **Environment:**
  - Unnatural cold with clean borders. Warmth that ends in a line.
  - Dead animals that sit composed and watch: the hare with no breath and
    no heartbeat shimmer, looking at you the way you look at someone you
    have been waiting for.
  - Ground and floorboards going deep matt black where the light is lowest.
  - Frost that patterns as growth rings, the grain of a thing split open.

- **People:**
  - The fear it sends is not the victim's own. It arrives from outside,
    too big and too fast, an instruction rather than a feeling.
  - Its attention has weight: patient, entire, settling over you like snow.

---

## Gameplay Role

- **The implemented game (v1)** stages the Act II climax as a fear spike, a
  run, and the collision, then the wrong layer. That broadly survives the
  rewrite: the encounter floods fear in from outside and the running takes
  Elli exactly where it wanted her.
- **What the rewrite changes** is everything after the false cabin door:
  the reunion is a deception, there is no acceptance ending, and the exit
  is refusal followed by indifference. Reconciliation of the mechanics is
  tracked in issue #141 (and #110 for the encounter's fear shape).
- **Constant across versions:** it appears indirectly (camera frames,
  tracks, arrangements, dreams). It is presence, attention, and wrongness.
  No stat screen, no in-world description that reduces it.

## Visual/Conceptual Imagery

- **Visuals:**
  - Never clearly seen. A tallness, leaning very slightly forward, like a
    thing that has been waiting a long time and does not mind.
  - A suggestion of a face, in the sense that a knot in bark suggests a
    face, wherever you are not quite looking.
  - The smell of split stone, and under it old smoke.

- **Conceptual Anchors:**
  - *Stillness.*
  - *Cold.*
  - *Deception.*
  - *Waiting.*
  - *Attention.*

---

## Final Thought

> It's not a monster in the woods.
> It's the warm room at the end of the running, and the friend inside who
> knows exactly how you take your coffee.
> That's the Lyer.
> And it's still there.
