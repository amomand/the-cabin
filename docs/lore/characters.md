# Characters – *The Cabin*

Creeping dread. Isolated Finnish wilderness. Secrets that sleep beneath the frost.

> Reconciled against the rewritten prose drafts of *The Cabin* and
> *No Further* (July 2026). The plotline snapshot in `plotline.md` is the
> beat-level source; this file carries who these people are.

## Elli

- **Nationality:** Finnish
- **Role:** Protagonist / Player POV of *The Cabin*
- **Background:**
  - Mid-forties. Grew up at the cabin; it is hers now, "on paper at least."
  - Left Finland after university. London first, then further, then further
    again. Twenty years abroad, publishing-adjacent in New York.
  - Pays the cabin electricity remotely, arranges maintenance through Nika,
    visits every few years and leaves before the quiet gets inside her.
- **Personality:**
  - Careful, observant. Manages feeling by arranging and fixing.
  - Her avoidance behaviour is the plot engine: she answers fear with tasks.
    A flight instead of a phone call is the kind of answer she is good at.
- **The estrangement:**
  - Twenty years of distance from Nika, made message by unsent message.
    Four years without a proper conversation. The missed funeral, flowers
    from an app, eleven listens of the voicemail answered with a text about
    a camera.
  - The estrangement is self-made and she knows it: "Nobody has looked at me
    the way you looked at me last night. Nobody has looked at me like that
    for twenty years. I made sure of it."
  - This is not backstory colour. It is the escape mechanism. The copy has
    never seen how the two women would actually stand in a room together,
    because that never happened anywhere. Speaking to the copy in the
    estranged register is what breaks the lie.
- **Arc:**
  - Consents to the comfortable lie (the coffee, the care, the bed), then
    does the accounting and owns her part: "I stayed the night. That part is
    mine."
  - In the coda she stops running. She sets the bag down, sits in her
    grandmother's chair facing the empty hook, and listens.

## Anike ("Nika")

- **Nationality:** Finnish
- **Role:** Protagonist / POV of *No Further*. In *The Cabin* she appears
  only by voicemail, texts, and one phone call.
- **Background:**
  - Never left. Works at Korpi Rauta, the hardware store in Korpikylä.
  - Keeps the cabin for Elli: the key under the north log, the latch on the
    wood store, the folded blankets stacked at the foot of the bed.
  - Keeps a years-old photograph of Elli taped to the monitor by the till.
    Elli saw it once and never said.
- **Personality:**
  - Direct, unsentimental, competent. Says things as she prices hinges, one
    after another, done. Her answer to fear is to reduce it to a task with a
    time attached.
  - The not-saying is one of the loves between her and Elli: words put down
    and carried instead.
- **In *No Further*:**
  - Investigates the cabin over repeated visits. Breaks the blue mug and
    carries the pieces home. Closes the green book the entity keeps leaving
    open. Gathers the village's fragments (still days, no further, let it be
    empty) and refuses the false invitation at the threshold with one word:
    "No."
  - Leaves the midnight voicemail that carries the naming: "It's... it's
    lying out there."
- **After *No Further*:**
  - Her real state stays an open wound. She counted the years of the
    estrangement in private; the copy spends that grief back at Elli in
    Act V.
  - The coda phone call gives her the pause with twenty years in it, the
    most human sound in the story. "Drive slow."

## The copy

- **Role:** The false Nika waiting in the false cabin. Acts III–V of
  *The Cabin*.
- **What it is:**
  - The entity wearing Nika exactly: the fleece with the bobbled Korpi Rauta
    logo, the cadence, the competence, the grip.
  - It is the *close* Nika, the friendship as Nika's grief carries it,
    whole. It cannot perform the estranged register back at Elli.
- **Knowledge rule (load-bearing):**
  - It knows only what Nika knows, feels, or witnessed, plus anything Elli
    says aloud to it. It knows the estrangement exists, counted out of
    Nika's grief. It has never seen the two women in a room together. That
    gap is the way out.
- **Tells:**
  - No doorway pause, no awkwardness, warmth that costs nothing.
  - Physical seams where Elli's belief leaks: frost grained like split wood,
    knuckles going birch for a breath, the smile laid on a half-beat late,
    breathing that never varies, floorboards going matt black where the
    light is lowest.
  - The impossible blue mug, whole, chip intact.

## Korpikylä (supporting cast, *No Further*)

- **Jukka**: Nika's colleague at Korpi Rauta. Tired, kind, will not go up
  the lake road and will not say why: "Not in November. Not past the lake
  road. Not during still days."
- **Matti**: in his seventies, at the bar. Keeper of his grandfather's 1934
  path ledger: distances that stop matching, *still days* in the margins,
  and the final entry for the northern path: *No further.*
- **Liisa**: at the petrol station. "My grandmother used to say if a place
  wants to be empty, let it."
- **Pekka**: at the bar. Furniture, in the way village bars have furniture
  that talks.

The village knows, in the way villages know: by what it will not say. One
man offers a name from folklore (*hiisi*) and Matti shuts it down flat. The
thing does not belong to any mythology, and the people closest to it refuse
to name it at all.

## Cut from canon

- **John** (Elli's American husband in an earlier draft) does not exist in
  the rewritten canon. Elli is alone, and the story requires it: nobody has
  been close enough to her in twenty years to give the copy the material.
  Do not reintroduce him.
