Beat reference for The Cabin, matched to the finished prose draft (The Cabin.md, July 2026). Written for adaptation into the text adventure: beats, states, objects and rules, nothing else. The prequel No Further is canon throughout.

## Premise

Elli returns alone from New York to the family cabin in northern Finland after Nika's voicemail and a second death of the northern camera. A maintenance errand walks her into the old woods, an encounter drives her south into a false cabin where a copy of Nika is waiting, and she escapes by speaking to the copy with the real twenty years of estrangement, which it cannot mirror. The creature lets her go with indifference. It is never named. Nika's line "It's lying out there" carries the naming.

## Rules (never stated in text, always obeyed)

- The creature wants willing participation inside a comfortable lie. It herds by fear only to make warmth land harder.
- The lie is co-authored. It holds exactly as well as Elli's belief holds. Seams (frost grain, wrong knuckles, late smile, black floorboards) appear where her belief leaks, not where the creature fails. The copy is at its most perfect when she is concussed and desperate.
- Knowledge rule: the copy knows only what Nika knows, feels, or witnessed (plus anything Elli says aloud to it). It knows the estrangement exists, having counted the years out of Nika's grief. What it has never seen is how the two women would actually behave in a room together, because that never happened anywhere. This gap is the escape mechanism.
- The copy is the close Nika, the friendship as Nika's grief carries it, whole. It cannot perform the estranged register back at Elli.
- Naming does not banish. Refusal spoils the game; the creature withdraws attention and releases her. No pursuit. The horror of the exit is indifference.
- Elli never sees the creature clearly. Three refused looks: after the collision, leaving the false cabin, and in never being shown what is under the face.
- The cost is implied only: it has now learnt Elli as it learnt Nika. The coda scraping is the receipt. Never articulated.

## Cast

- Elli. Mid-forties, twenty years abroad, publishing-adjacent in New York. Careful, observant, manages feeling by arranging and fixing. Her avoidance behaviour is the plot engine: she answers fear with tasks.
- Nika. In Korpikylä, at the hardware store. Appears only by voicemail, texts and one phone call. Her real state after No Further stays an open wound.
- The copy. Wears Nika exactly: fleece, cadence, competence, the grip. Tells: no doorway pause, no awkwardness, warmth that costs nothing, plus the physical seams above.

## Timeline (fixed numbers)

- Six weeks before arrival: fox photo, "Your fox learnt to fly."
- Five weeks: the five frames, second death of the northern camera. "Could be a deer" call; Nika drives up (the events of No Further).
- Eleven days: Nika's midnight voicemail, received Friday afternoon New York time. Elli replies with a maintenance text only.
- Seven days: camera blurs and dies a third time. Next morning Elli books a flight, earliest seat five days out. She tells no one.
- Day zero: Tuesday, 16:10, she drives through Korpikylä past the lit shop without stopping.

## Act I: the real evening

1. Arrival. Rental parked at the road end, walk up the drive, key under the north log in black plastic. Power out; reset at the fusebox in the porch.
2. Memory beats on entry: the childhood scraping her parents explained away (she knew it was inside, never told them); grandmother's "before the forest moved".
3. Reopening rituals: wood, fire, water, bedding. The hook by the stove is empty. The blue mug is gone (reader of No Further knows it is broken and binned; Elli assumes Nika moved it). She takes a white mug of her own.
4. Sauna at dusk, the one moment the distance to the place closes.
5. Dinner, wine from the airport bottle, glass finished, bottle corked on the counter.
6. Voicemail re-listen at the window (one bar): "Elli. It's me. You need to come home. Something's wrong with the cabin. I don't know what. Don't go up on your own. Wait. It's... it's lying out there." The pause before the last line frightens her most. Backstory: the deleted reply ("Call me tonight, whatever time"), the sent one ("OK. Don't go up again. I'll sort the camera from here.").
7. Five-frames rewatch. She knows them by heart. Frame four: the forked birch has moved from right of frame to left of centre. Frame five black.
8. Bed. Isolation total but not hostile. Plan: check the camera in daylight.

## Act II: morning and the woods

1. Waking to complete silence; a hearth log restores sound.
2. Fox tracks fifty metres out: a clean trotting line that stops mid-stride, last print perfect, nothing beyond. She stands where Nika stood.
3. Camera maintenance (her avoidance made physical). Battery reads full but dead; new battery, instant green light. Live feed against frame one: the forked birch now stands left of centre, and nearer.
4. The errand extends itself: she walks in to check the birch. Kit: head torch, compass clipped to her jacket, half a day of light. The birch grows from unbroken ground that it did not occupy five weeks ago. Looking back, the cabin is gone behind young spruce.
5. Deterioration: grey needles, sap-dead trees, no animal life at any distance.
6. The hare: sitting in the open on the path, composed, no breath, no heartbeat shimmer, looking at her the way you look at someone you have been waiting for. She passes it slowly and does not look back.
7. Old woods: canopy knitted shut, cold rising from the ground, smell of split stone and old smoke. The deer path no longer exists. She turns for home.
8. Encounter: wall of cold, absolute quiet, presence behind her. She turns. It is close enough to touch, seen only in fragments, and it is looking at her the way you look at someone you have been waiting for (deliberate echo of the hare). Fear floods in from outside: run, south.
9. Collision with a pine at full speed. Winded, concussed, ribs, nosebleed. The stillness thickens beside her as something settles to look. Animal reflex gets her up. First refused look. She runs south as the light fails an hour early.
10. A clearing opens: the cabin, one window lit warm. She throws herself at the door.

## Act III: the false evening

Mirrors Act I beat for beat, accompanied. Every wrongness lands inside warmth.

1. She falls into heat. Fire hours old and tended, towel warming, mug waiting on the table. It reads as rescue, not strangeness.
2. Nika is at the table with the green book. On her feet in the first second, grip on Elli's arm, no doorway pause at all. "Christ. What happened to you?"
3. The lie, load-bearing: "You called me. So I drove up. Door was open." Elli never called anyone. Concussion stops her checking; the thought sinks under the kettle.
4. Care: face cleaned, pupils checked, ribs pressed. Verdict: concussion, cracked or bruised ribs, "you're not walking anywhere tonight. First light, we walk out together."
5. The coffee arrives in the blue mug, chip at two o'clock of the handle, made exactly right without asking. Her thumb finds the chip on its own. She nearly cries. (The hook was empty last night; the thought lifts its head and is filed away.)
6. Seam one: frost on the window branching as growth rings, wood grain. Blinked away.
7. Dinner from tins, easy talk, no gap to work across where twenty years should sit. It does not hurt, and she is too tired and grateful to ask why.
8. Seam two: the hand on the plate goes birch-bark for a breath, knots for knuckles. "You're staring." "Sorry. Concussion."
9. Elli opens the door looking for the cars. No drive, no vehicles, black ground, wrong ancient treeline, starless flat ceiling that feels near. The copy, calm at her shoulder: "First light. We'll walk out at first light, together, on the compass. Come inside. I'm here now." Word for word what the real Nika would say. She lets the door close. The consent beat.
10. Sauna deferred ("you'd cook your brain"), the spare mattress laid by the narrow bed, "Like when we were kids." The copy sleeps between her and the door, where Nika always lived.
11. In the dark, the copy narrates Nika's treasured memory (the towel, the lake path, "You never once looked back... you knew I'd be there"). True, and a thing the real Nika would die before saying aloud. First conscious itch.

## Act IV: the knowing (same night)

1. The breathing below her never varies. Forty identical breaths. The hare's stilled flank, inverted.
2. The inventory assembles: frost grain, knuckles, late smile, the impossible mug, "you called me" against a memory that contains no call.
3. New seams she gathers deliberately: her phone will not wake (dark all through, like the sky); the dinner tins were never hers; there is no wine anywhere in this cabin.
4. The flaw beneath all of it: it doesn't hurt. The real Nika would have come, but with a beat at the threshold, boots half unlaced, both of them deciding how to stand. The thing knows the years (counted out of Nika's grief) but has never seen the room where those years would stand between them. Nobody has. She made sure of it.
5. As her belief withdraws, the room leaks: fire too low, warmth pulled back to a clean border, floorboards going matt black where the light is lowest. When looked at directly, boards. The room holds its shape from her attention.
6. She does not sleep. The accounting: she drank the coffee, let herself be settled, lay in the bed it made, wanting it. That part is hers.

## Act V: the refusal and the walk out

1. Wrong grey dawn. The copy rises without waking and offers coffee in the blue mug. "Drink up. We'll want the light."
2. "No. Thank you." Register change: level, courteous, spaced, a woman speaking to someone she does not know well. Small talk of the estranged: "It was good of you to drive up... a long way to come for someone else's problem."
3. The copy tries a purchase and finds none. "Elli. It's me."
4. The estrangement spoken, behavioural, no mechanism explained: four years without a proper conversation, the missed funeral, flowers from an app, eleven listens answered with a text about a camera, the photograph taped to the monitor by the till that she saw and never mentioned. "She doesn't look at me the way you looked at me last night. Nobody has looked at me like that for twenty years. I made sure of it."
5. The kettle stops hissing without being moved. The copy stops lying and speaks Nika's grief back at her, flat, all of it true and all of it Nika's: she counted the years, fourteen since Elli slept a night here, she never once asked Elli to come home until the message, and Elli wrote back about a camera.
6. Elli completes the voicemail: "She told me one more thing. On the message. She said, it's lying out there." Then: "She warned me. I came anyway. I stayed the night. That part is mine. And you are still not her."
7. The pretence stops mid-stance, as an actor stops when the take is over. Face no longer worn from inside. Fire grey, black up the walls to the sills, frost pattern complete, warmth ending at a clean line. Attention withdraws like a book being closed. Nothing in the cabin is interested in her any more.
8. She dresses, does not look at what stands in Nika's fleece (final refused look), and walks out into the black on the compass. The woods do not arrange themselves. No pursuit. The worst hour: mattering to nothing. She falls twice; the second time her ribs decide when she gets up.
9. Real frost returns patchily. A mass of snow falls somewhere: the first sound the world has made in hours. Daylight with a direction. She crosses her own day-old boot prints and comes out fifty metres from the wood store. The real cabin, dead windows, no smoke.

## Coda

1. Inside: cold, yesterday's fire, bed open where she left it, corked bottle and empty glass, and the hook by the stove empty. She stands in front of the hook a while.
2. Phone call at the window. Four rings. "Elli." Then the pause with twenty years in it, the most human sound she has ever heard. "You went up." "Yes." "Alone. I told you to wait." "I know." Then, quieter: "There's coffee at the shop. Come down before the light goes. Drive slow past the lake." "I'm coming down. Niks." "Drive slow."
3. She is packing when the scraping begins. Under the boards, slow, rhythmic, the childhood sound. She knows it now: not something trying to get in, something letting her know it is there.
4. It moved her once and the running took her exactly where it wanted her. She sets the bag down, sits in her grandmother's chair facing the empty hook, hands flat on the wood, and listens.
5. The scraping goes on for a while. Then it stops. She waits. End.

## Objects and their states (for the game)

- Blue mug: real cabin hook empty (destroyed in No Further) → whole in the false cabin, chip intact → hook empty again in the coda. The single best tell; never explained to Elli.
- Voicemail: heard in New York, re-listened at the cabin, quoted to the copy at the climax. Carries the naming.
- Five frames: rewatch object, not discovery. Frame four holds the moved birch.
- Forked birch: right of frame five weeks ago, left of centre and nearer now, unbroken ground at its roots.
- Phone: one bar at the real cabin window only; will not wake inside the false cabin.
- Compass: clipped to her jacket from the Act II walk; the copy names it; it is her exit tool.
- Key: under the north log at arrival, in her pocket thereafter.
- Green book: open under the copy's hand when Elli falls through the false door. Load-bearing for readers of both stories: in *No Further* the entity keeps leaving the book open and Nika pointedly closes it without reading, so the copy sits doing the one thing the real Nika refused to do, the entity's own habit showing through the costume. Keep it open; never comment on it.
- Photograph (No Further): taped to the monitor by the till at Korpi Rauta; Elli saw it once and never said.
- Wine bottle and glass: real cabin only. Absence in the false cabin is a tell.
- Fox tracks and hare: one-way tells in the woods, never re-encountered.
